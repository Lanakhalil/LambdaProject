Tritanopia

A very rare color vision disturbance in which only the red and the green cone pigments are present, with a total absence of blue retinal receptors. tritanopia and tritanomaly are not sex-linked traits and can be acquired rather than inherited and can be reversed in some cases.

Deuteranopia

A type of red-green color blindness characterized by the inability to distinguish red and green pigments. It is inherited and caused by recessive genes in the X chromosome.

Living a Healthy, Colorful and Considerate Life

Our eyes are as precious as diamonds, we should always treat them as such. With the increase of going online and finishing everything remotely, it is important to take some measures to keep your eyes away from exhaustion. One important tip is to limit exposure to ulrtraviolet and intense light, you can do so by limiting your screen time. Always eat your vegetables, they are important for the production of elements used in photochemical reactions and creating nervous pulses to the brain.

Just like our eyes, fellow humans are just as precious. It is so important to keep in mind people who are color blind, some might have gone through this because of medications or disease. It is very important to take them into consideration and creating tools that make their lives easier, because they are already missing much. One amazing gadget is color-sight glasses that might be amazing but a little expensive.
