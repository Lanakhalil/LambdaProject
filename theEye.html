The Human Eye

Humans have a complicated trichromatic color vision system.The main component of this fascinating system is the eye.It captures photos like a camera, in fact cameras are built on the eye's anatomy!

The normal human eye basically consists of cornea, behind which are a clear liquid (the aqueous humor), a variable aperture (the pupil) and the crystalline.These components all work together to form an image that eventually reaches the retina, in which there are photoreceptive cells.
The retina has cone cells and rod cells, which have pigmented photorecptors that go through a photochemical reaction that ends with forming an electric voltage that reaches the brain and gets translated as a picture.
Images are formed in the eye as follows, and it is the responsibility of the brain to translate these converted images.

Errors on the front part of the eye can cause refractive errors (related to the focus of the image, size, far point, etc) and errors on the back part (retina, optical nerve) can cause adaption errors and color deficiencies.
